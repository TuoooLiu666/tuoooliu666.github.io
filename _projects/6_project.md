My active research projects involving frontline workers and COVID-19 using proteomics are listed below:

- Serum Proteomics in high-exposure individuals to novel COVID-19: Normal vs. Long-COVID
  - aims: to investigate differences in the proteome for frontline and healthcare workers afflicted with long COVID as compared to those with normal COVID-19 before, during, and after infection.
  - data source: the Arizona Healthcare, Emergency Response, and Other Essential Workers Surveillance Study (<a href="https://azheroes.arizona.edu/">AZ HEROES</a>)
  - contribution: led statistical analyses & drafted manuscript & publication
  - status: manuscript in preparation

- Serum Proteomics in Omicron SARS-CoV-2 breakthrough: a nested case-control study within a prospective cohort of frontline workers from eight locations in the United States (US).
  - aims: to investigate differences in frontline workers' proteomic profiles comparing those with breakthrough infection and those without.
  - data source: the Arizona Healthcare, Emergency Response, and Other Essential Workers Surveillance Study (<a href="https://azheroes.arizona.edu/">AZ HEROES</a>)
  - contribution: proteomics analysis
  - status: manuscript in preparation
